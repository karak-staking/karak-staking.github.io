How it works

Karak Staking in three steps

Start with the wallet and asset you already use. The app keeps the path readable, shows variable network costs before signing, and sends every transaction back to your wallet for approval.

Step 01

Connect your wallet

Open the app and connect a self-custody wallet such as MetaMask. You keep the private keys and approve each transaction yourself.

Step 02

Choose asset and chain

Select ETH or a supported liquid staking token, then confirm the active network. The interface is built for Ethereum staking users who want a restaking route with clear wallet prompts.

Step 03

Review and sign

Check the live quote, gas estimate, receiving asset, and contract interaction in the app. If the details match your intent, sign from your wallet to create or update the position.

Why Karak

Put staked assets to work without giving up custody

Restaking-ready flow

Karak Staking is framed for users who want ETH and supported LST exposure to remain productive across DeFi. The app surfaces the active route before you sign instead of burying it after the fact.

Live terms, no stale promises

Reward estimates, asset availability, route details, and gas are shown live in the app. That matters for staking because rates and costs move with protocol conditions and network demand.

Built for wallet control

FAQ

Karak Staking questions

01

What is Karak Staking?

Karak Staking is a non-custodial DeFi staking and restaking app for users who want to put ETH or supported liquid staking assets to work from their own wallet.

02

What assets can I use?

The app is built around ETH and supported LST routes, with stETH shown as a primary staking asset where available. Check the app before signing because asset availability can change.

03

How long does staking take?

Timing depends on wallet approval, network congestion, and the selected route. The app shows the transaction state while the network processes your action.

04

Is Karak Staking non-custodial?

Yes. You use your own wallet, keep your private keys, and approve transactions directly. Never enter a seed phrase into any staking page or dashboard.

05

What does it cost?

You pay the applicable network gas and any route-specific costs shown before confirmation. Use a live source such as the Ethereum gas tracker to understand current network conditions.

06

Which chains are supported?

Karak Staking displays active chain support inside the app, and this site maintains a plain-language overview on the supported chains page.
